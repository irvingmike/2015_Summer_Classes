But, wait! That's not the only way. What if the numbers coming back in the tuple have a meaning? Something like minimum and maximum values in an array? We could do something like this.

1
2
let minimumElement = aTuple.0
let maximumElement = aTuple.1

But, we don't have to, Swift can do it for us.

1
2
3
let (minimumElement, maximumElement) = twoNumbers()

println("The minimum is \(minimumElement), the maximum is \(maximumElement)")

How Big?

How many elements can we have in a Tuple? Any number we want. However, they are more usable and manageable if the number is smaller.
